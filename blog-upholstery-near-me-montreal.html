Where to Get Furniture Reupholstered
in Montreal

What to look for, what to ask, and what separates a shop worth trusting from one worth avoiding. From Atlas Upholstering, serving Montreal since 1967.

Montreal March 30, 2026 By Atlas Upholstering ~5 min read

Searching for "upholstery near me" in Montreal will surface a range of options — from solo craftspeople working out of garages to established studios with decades of experience. Not all upholstery shops are equal, and the wrong choice can mean a long wait, disappointing results, or a piece that doesn't hold up.

Here's what to look for — and what to ask — before you hand over a piece of furniture.

What to look for in a Montreal upholstery shop

Years in business and verified reviews

Upholstery is a skilled trade. A shop that has been operating for decades has solved thousands of problems and built a reputation based on real results. Look for genuine Google reviews — not just a handful, but a consistent pattern of satisfied customers. A 4.8 or 4.9-star rating across 30+ reviews means something; a 5.0 based on four reviews means very little.

In-house fabric selection

A quality upholstery shop carries fabric in-house so you can see, touch, and compare options in person. If a shop can only show you a catalogue or a handful of swatches, your choices will be limited and you won't know what the fabric really looks like on the piece. Look for a shop with 100+ upholstery-grade fabrics — velvets, linens, performance weaves, leathers — and the knowledge to guide your selection based on durability and use case.

Honest turnaround times

A good shop gives you a realistic timeline and sticks to it. Be cautious of shops that quote unusually fast turnarounds — quality upholstery takes time. For most residential pieces, 2–4 weeks from fabric confirmation is reasonable. Rush jobs done in a few days are a red flag.

Pickup and delivery

Moving a sofa or sectional is a major undertaking. A full-service upholstery shop offers pickup and delivery so you're not stuck renting a truck or doing multiple trips. Confirm this is available in your area when requesting a quote.

Transparent pricing and written estimates

You should always receive a written estimate before work begins — no exceptions. The estimate should detail fabric cost, labour, any foam or structural repairs, and pickup/delivery if applicable. Any shop that can't give you a clear written quote before starting work is a risk.

Questions to ask before choosing an upholstery shop

  • How long have you been in business? Experience matters in a skilled trade.
  • How many fabrics do you carry in-house? Can I come in to view and select fabric in person?
  • What is your typical turnaround time for a project like mine?
  • Do you offer pickup and delivery? What areas do you serve?
  • Do you work on antique or heirloom pieces? Do you do frame repairs in-house?
  • Will you provide a written estimate before starting?
  • What is your warranty on workmanship?
